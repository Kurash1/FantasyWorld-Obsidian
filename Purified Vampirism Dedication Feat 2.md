(Rare) (Archetype) (Dedication)
**Archetype** Purified Vampirism
**Prerequisite** You died while one or two purified vampires drank from your blood or you have been purified of your rotting nature in a ritual.

--- 
You're a newly risen purified vampire. You gain the vampire trait and the basic purified benefits, and your hunger is for the blood of the regular living. Your incisors elongate; you gain a fangs unarmed attack that deals 1d6 piercing damage. They're in t he brawling group and have the grapple and unarmed traits. You gain the Drink Blood ability. Unlike a regular vampire however you do not gain the undead trait as the purified nature of your new existence is fully vitamantic in practice making you essentially quasi-alive. If you gained this archetype via being purified by a ritual you lose the vampire archetype and replace it with this one. 

**Drink Blood** (one-action) (divine), (necromancy) 
**Requirements** A grabbed, paralyzed, restrained, unconscious, or willing creature is within your reach; **Effect** You sink your fangs into that creature and drink its blood. This requires an Athletics check against the victim's Fortitude DC if the victim is grabbed, and automatically succeeds for any of the other conditions. If you succeed, the creature becomes drained 1, and you gain temporary HP equal to the target's level that last for 10 minutes. Further uses against the target don't increase the drained condition or grant you more temporary HP.  
  
**Special** You can't select another dedication feat until you have gained two other feats from the vampire archetype.

Basic Purified Benefits
The purified vampirism archetype gives the basic purified benefits detailed here.

**Necril**: You know the Necril language.
**Purified Vision**: You gain low-light vision, or you gain darkvision if your ancestry already has low-light vision.
**Positive Healing**: Due to your vitamantic nature you are healed by vitality effects. You take damage from void effects. 
**Positive Survival**: Unlike a regular vampire, you aren't destroyed when reduced to 0 hit points. Instead your vitamantic nature ensures that you roll death saving throws as normal and benefit from many methods to bringing someone back from dying, such as stabilize.
**Immunity to Death Effects**: You're immune to death effects. This keeps you from being automatically killed or from having your dying value automatically increase, but it doesn't make you immune to other parts of the spell or effect. For example, you can still take mental damage and become frightened by a phantasmal killer, you just don't instantly die from it.  
**Disease and Poison Protection**: You gain a +1 circumstance bonus to saving throws (or any other defense) against disease and poison.  
**Undead Hunger**: While you don't eat or drink the same food as humanoids do, you usually have thirsts and hungers that resembles the regular state of an undead, such as a ghoul's hunger for flesh, a zombie's craving for brains, and a vampire's desire for blood. Additionally, while you don't sleep, you enter a state of quiescence for at least 4 hours a day to recuperate, which lets your undead flesh reknit and recover naturally. Many undead choose to rest when the sun is at its highest.
**Contradictory Edicts and Anathema**: Due to your unnatural existence that drags away from both sides the living and the dead the universe bars you with a cost. The GM determines Edicts and Anathema that you cannot ever break. These must somewhat contradict itself but not so much that you cannot find loopholes around it. Should you ever not fulfil and edict or break an anathema you instantly fall unconscious and start rolling death saves. In addition to this you instantly gain negative conditions the GM deems fitting for the type of failure you produced. These conditions stay until you remedied your failure in some way. The GM determines how to remedy them. It must however be possible!
**Immortality**: You cannot die of natural causes.
