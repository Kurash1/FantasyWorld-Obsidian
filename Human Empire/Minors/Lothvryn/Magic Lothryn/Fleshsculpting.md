
Fleshsculpting is a procedure that remodels a subjects into a myriad of different forms. It is widely considered as subtype of fleshwarping. It differs in it so much that it attempts to achieve mainly aesthetically pleasing results to the client with practical concerns being largely regarded as secondary. Practitioners therefore view themselves rather as artists than anything else. The procedure is practised all over the world but is most prominently elevated in [[Lothvryn]], thanks to the esoteric magical occurrences the realm already experiences thus leading to many children being born altered, and in [[Astviel]] where ancient covens and other organisations practice this profession. Thus it can occur naturally but more conceptual ways to achieve this exist as well. Those include but are not limited to grafting, alchemical and magical procedures, as well as mundane surgical procedures. The limits of this form of reshaping are rather unknown and often tightly guarded by any fleshsculpter. But a few observations can be made some people who underwent the procedure now posses features reminiscent of serpentine, avian or even aquatic beings. A myriad of other forms however also  exist. The Elixir of Gender Transformation is also said by some scholars to be a form of fleshsculpting. 

Lamian Surgery
(Uncommon) (Graft) (Magical)
Usage: fused
Price: X gp
This magical fusion of legs into a serpentine tail is popular among nobles in Lothvryn, who spend long hours standing at diet meetings and other court events. After undergoing this graft of fleshsculpting art, the patient losers their legs, which are fused into serpentine tail.  You gain a +1 untyped bonus to make an impression with your ancestry or ancestries similar enough to yours that they qualify. The GM decides if an Ancestry qualifies for this bonus. In addition you no longer qualify for ancestry or heritage feats that require legs to function. However you now do qualify for Nagaji feats that require a tail to function. The GM decides which feats qualify. 

Special: Due to this Graft replacing one thing for another it does not have the invested trait as a Graft usually would have. 

Mermaidification
(Rare) (Graft) (Magical)
Usage: fused
Price: X gp
The most refined fleshsculpting procedure currently known in the world. It is often undertaken by patients who absolutely detest being on land and long for the weightlessness of drifting in water or by adventurers who spend most of their time in aquatic environments. After undergoing this graft of fleshsculpting art, the patient loses their legs, which are fused into dolphin-like tail. In addition new organs, webbed skin and fins are implanted to help with buoyancy. You however do not obtain any gills from this and have to pursue a separate graft for this.  Your land speed becomes 5ft and you obtain a 25ft swim speed. You no longer qualify for ancestry or heritage feats that require legs to function. However you now do qualify for certain Merfolk feats whose biological necessities you meet. If you want to pick the Shore Gift feat the GM might decide you have to spend active time researching the old magic it uses. 

Special: Due to this Graft replacing one thing for another it does not have the invested trait as a Graft usually would have.
