[[Watar#History]]
Watar is actually just a lose collection of semi independent watarian tribes who have various different customary legal systems. It formed shortly after the age of collapse out of remnants of a long forgotten valley tribe confederation and the humans and elves already living in that forest. It became one of the electors of the empire as the grand duchy of [[Vorn]] envisioned. And had the purpose of representing the minor realms of the empire due to its highly decentralised nature. A function it still fulfills to this day. During the [[Guilder War]] war it sided against the arch duchy of [[Veshni]]. Citing that the regent did not uphold the [[Elven Settlement Treaty]]  Which was a vital treaty for Watar as it gave the [[watarians]] a lot of what they wanted. First the recongition that the the semi-watarians in [[Vorn]] may participate in internal watarian affairs. Second that the backwater of northern Watar would be settled by the [[Rose Elves]] who would aid in ensuring economic success and public order in that underpopulated backwater, third that the new duchy of silkenglade is to be confederated with Watar and lastly that Watar would get legal claims on the parts of the watarian forest that left the empire during the violan collapse. After the War it was negotiated that Watar's rights from the treaty would be acknowledged thus cutting its steadfast allegiance to [[Vorn]] for the time being. 

Additional Information:
- Semi-Watarians in the [[Thornwoods]] and [[Rosewoods]] use [[vornari]] codified law rather than the customary system other [[watarians]] utilise. 
- Watar once attempted to introduce a legal and language reform called [[New Watarian]] which failed to be accepted by the huscarls and druids of the country due to concerns about cultural traditions and timid implementation attempts.
- [[Vorn]] and [[Watar]] rule the [[Thornwoods]] and [[Rosewoods]] as a sort of condominium. [[Vornari]] law applies but the customs of the [[watarians]] are likewise legally recognised and they are free to participate in Watar's affairs.
